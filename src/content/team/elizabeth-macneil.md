Elizabeth MacNeil is a registered occupational therapist with experience supporting individuals across the lifespan, from pediatrics to geriatrics. She currently works in long-term care, providing compassionate support to older adults to enhance their quality of life.

She also works with autistic children and families through FSCD contracts, focusing on augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) systems and individualized support. Her practice is rooted in neurodiversity-affirming and client-centered approaches.

Elizabeth integrates assistive technology, sensory strategies, and motor skill development to support communication, self-care, and daily participation. She collaborates closely with families and multidisciplinary teams to design meaningful, personalized interventions.

In addition to clinical work, she provides coaching, public speaking, and training for parents, therapists, and organizations, advocating for inclusive and strengths-based care.
