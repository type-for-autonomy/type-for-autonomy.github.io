Vikram Jaswal is a Professor of Psychology at the University of Virginia and the director of the Jaswal Lab. His work focuses on cognitive development, communication, and social interaction in children and autistic individuals.

Earlier in his career, Dr. Jaswal studied topics such as word learning, categorization, memory development, and social cognition. His research later shifted toward understanding communication in nonspeaking autistic individuals and identifying ways to support their ability to express themselves through alternative communication methods.

Recent work from his lab has shown that many nonspeaking autistic individuals possess foundational literacy skills, suggesting that typing-based communication could provide an effective alternative to speech. His team has developed experimental methods to measure spelling knowledge and orthographic understanding using tablet-based interaction tasks.

Dr. Jaswal’s research has significantly influenced discussions about communication rights and literacy opportunities for nonspeaking autistic individuals. His work continues to explore how assistive technologies and educational approaches can support written communication and improve accessibility for neurodivergent individuals.
